The President, after consulting with the Prime Minister and the Speakers of the Parliament, may declare the dissolution of the National Assembly.
General elections are held at least twenty days and no later than forty days after the dissolution.
The National Assembly is legally formed on the second Friday after the elections. If this session is held outside the regular session period, a special session will be held for a period of fifteen days. The dissolution of the National Assembly is not permitted in the year following these elections.
