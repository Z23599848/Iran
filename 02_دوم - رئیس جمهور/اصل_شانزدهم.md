Whenever the Republic's territorial integrity, national independence, or international commitments are seriously threatened, and the regular performance of the aforementioned public powers in the Constitution is disrupted, the President shall take necessary measures, after official consultation with the Prime Minister, the heads of the legislative bodies, and the head of the Constitutional Council, to remedy the situation.

The President shall inform the public of the situation through an announcement.
These measures must be taken in a manner that enables the aforementioned public powers in the Constitution to carry out their duties in the shortest possible time. In this regard, the President shall consult with the Constitutional Council and call for a parliamentary session to address the issue.
The National Assembly cannot be dissolved during the exercise of these extraordinary powers.
