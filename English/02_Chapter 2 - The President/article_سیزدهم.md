The President signs the guidelines and regulations approved by the Council of Ministers.
He appoints military and national officials.
The government's advisors, the secretary of the Society for the Award of Honors, ambassadors and extraordinary envoys, advisors to the Court of Accounts, governors, senior officers and heads of academies, and managers of central offices are appointed to the Council of Ministers.
The fundamental law defines other professions that are appointed by the Council of Ministers and the conditions under which the power of appointment for national and military professions can be delegated to another person to be executed in the name of the President.
