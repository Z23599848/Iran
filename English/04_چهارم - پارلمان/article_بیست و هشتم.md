The President guarantees the independence of the Judiciary.
The High Council of the Judiciary assists him in this matter.
The Judiciary is formed in accordance with the Basic Law.
Judges are sitting members and cannot be removed or replaced.

ðŸ”¹Article 65-1

The head of the Supreme Council of Judges is the President. The Minister of Justice is its Vice President according to the law. He can also be the President's deputy.
The Supreme Council of Judges consists of two bodies, one of which is authorized to deal with sitting judges, and the other with judges of the courts.
The competent authority regarding sitting judges, in addition to the President and the Minister of Justice, consists of five sitting judges, one court judge, a government advisor appointed by the Cabinet, and three personalities who are neither members of parliament nor affiliated with the judiciary, appointed respectively by the President, the Speaker of the National Assembly, and the Speaker of the Senate.
The competent authority regarding judges of the courts, in addition to the President and the Minister of Justice, consists of five court judges, one sitting judge, a government advisor, and the three personalities mentioned in the previous paragraph.

ðŸ”¹Article 65-2

The Supreme Council of Judges proposes appointments of sitting judges to the Chief Justice of the Supreme Court, the Head of the State Dispute Resolution Council, and the heads of emergency courts. Other sitting judges are appointed with the favorable opinion of this council.
This body acts as the disciplinary supervisory council for sitting judges. At this time, its chairmanship is with the Chief Justice of the Supreme Court.
The Supreme Council of Judges, with the exception of appointments that are determined by the Council of Ministers, expresses its opinion on the appointment of judges of the courts.
This body also expresses its opinion on the executive discipline guarantee for judges of the courts. The Prosecutor General is the head of this council. An organizational law determines the conditions for the implementation of this principle.
