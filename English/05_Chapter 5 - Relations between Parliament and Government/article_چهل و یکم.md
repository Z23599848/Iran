The right to revise the Constitution jointly belongs to the President, the Prime Minister, and the members of the Parliament.

The revision bill or proposal must be approved with the same text in both Houses of Parliament. The revision will be definitive through a referendum after its approval.

However, in case the President decides to present the revision bill to the Parliament, which holds a joint session, it will not be put to a general vote; in this case, the revision bill will only be approved by obtaining a majority (three-fifths) of the total votes cast. The joint session office will be the same as that of the National Assembly.

When the territorial integrity of the country is endangered, revising the Constitution or pursuing it is impossible.

The form of the Republican government cannot be subject to revision.
