ğŸ”¹Article 68 - Clause 1

Government officials are responsible for any criminal or tortious acts committed in the course of their duties. They will be tried by the Judiciary of the Islamic Republic of Iran. The Judiciary of the Islamic Republic of Iran is bound by the definitions of crimes and torts, as well as the determination of the penalties resulting from the laws.

ğŸ”¹Article 68 - Clause 2

The Judiciary of the Islamic Republic of Iran consists of fifteen judges: twelve members of parliament, elected by an equal number from the National Assembly and the Senate, after each general or partial renewal of these assemblies, and three judges of the Supreme Court, one of whom presides over the Judiciary of the Islamic Republic of Iran. Anyone who claims to have been harmed by a crime or tort committed by a government official in the course of his duties can file a complaint with an investigative commission. This commission issues an order to enforce the verdict or to refer it to the Prosecutor General of the Supreme Court for referral to the Judiciary of the Islamic Republic of Iran. The Prosecutor General of the Supreme Court may also refer it to the Judiciary of the Islamic Republic of Iran at his discretion with the consent of the investigative commission. The Fundamental Law determines the conditions for the implementation of this article.
